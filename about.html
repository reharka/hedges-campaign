Sara Hedges was raised in Okeechobee. She is the daughter of Robbie Hedges and Georganna Kirk, the granddaughter of Jim and Zella Kirk, and the granddaughter of R.G. and Christine Hedges. She has a sister, Robyn Harka, who now lives in Raleigh, North Carolina and a brother, Bradly Kirk. Sara’s mother is the Chief Administrative Officer for Florida Community Health Centers. Her grandparents, Jim and Zella Kirk, are both retired from the Okeechobee School Board. Jim Kirk also recently retired from the Okeechobee City Council, where he served as Mayor of Okeechobee for 26 years. R.G. Hedges served in the United States Army and retired from the railroad, after working for CSX Transportation.

Sara graduated from Okeechobee High School in 2005 as the co-Valedictorian of her class. During high school, Sara was a member of the Beta Club, National Honor Society, Latin Club, and Student Council. She served as the Beta Club President, the National Honor Society Vice-President, and was the Student Council Secretary.


After high school, Sara went on to attend Florida State University, receiving her Bachelor of Science degree in 2009. She graduated Suma Cum Laude from FSU with a double major in Political Science and Sociology. While at FSU, Sara was a member of the National Society of Collegiate Scholars, the Phi Alpha Delta Pre-Law Fraternity, the Golden Key International Honour Society, and the Phi Kappa Phi Honor Society. She had the honor of making the Dean’s List during three different semesters and the President’s List during four different semesters.


Upon graduating from FSU, Sara went on to receive her Juris Doctor degree from Mercer University School of Law, graduating Cum Laude in 2012. During law school, Sara was a member of the Mercer University Law Review, the Association of Women Law Students, and the Mock Trial Board of Mercer Advocacy Council. She and her Mock Trial Partner competed at the National Trial Competition in the Memphis Regionals. Sara received multiple honors while in law school, including receiving the CALI Excellence for the Future Award in Advanced Legal Research and being selected for the Dean’s List during three different semesters.

During law school, Sara returned home for the summers of 2010 and 2011 to intern in the Okeechobee Office of the State Attorney’s Office of the 19 th Judicial Circuit of Florida. Upon graduating from law school in May of 2012, she took the bar exam in August of 2012, received her passing results and was sworn in as an Attorney in Florida in September of 2012 in Okeechobee County Courthouse by Judge Jerald D. Bryant.


In October of 2012, Sara began working as an Assistant State Attorney for the 19 th Judicial Circuit of Florida. For over five years, Sara has prosecuted both misdemeanor and felony criminal offenses for the State of Florida. She has practiced in both County Court and Circuit Court. As an Assistant State Attorney, Sara has prosecuted over 50 jury trials for a wide variety of offenses, including Murder, Attempted Murder, Robbery, Lewd and Lascivious Molestation, Burglary, Aggravated Battery, Dealing in Stolen Property, Fleeing and Eluding, Grand Theft, Driving Under the Influence, Petit Theft, and Battery. As an Assistant State Attorney, Sara is responsible for a felony case load of over 200 cases. She is responsible for the filing decisions, motions, hearings, pleas, trials, and violations of probation for all cases assigned to her. Sara has argued numerous Motions to Suppress, argued Stand Your Ground Hearings, and written responses to Appellate Motions. Additionally, as an Assistant State Attorney, Sara regularly reviews search warrants and arrest affidavits for probable cause before submission to a Judge.
